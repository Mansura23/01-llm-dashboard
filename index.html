What is an LLM?

LLM stands for Large Language Model. It is a type of artificial intelligence trained on huge amounts of text data to understand and generate human language.

LLMs work by predicting the next token in a sequence of text. During training, the model learns patterns, grammar, facts, and relationships between words from books, websites, articles, and many other datasets.

Some popular examples of LLMs are GPT-4o by OpenAI, Claude 3.5 Sonnet by Anthropic, Gemini by Google, and Llama by Meta.

What is a Token?

A token is a small chunk of text used by language models. Tokens are usually around 3-4 characters or about 3/4 of a word in English.

Tokens matter because they are used to measure context window size and API pricing. The more tokens a request uses, the more expensive it becomes.

Example: The sentence "Hello, world!" may be split into tokens like: "Hello", ",", "world", "!".

English usually consumes fewer tokens compared to Azerbaijani because English words are often shorter and more common in training datasets. Azerbaijani words may split into more tokens due to suffixes and language structure.
